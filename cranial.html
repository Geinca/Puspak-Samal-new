Cranial Treatments

Cranial Treatment, also known as Craniosacral Therapy (CST) or Cranial Osteopathy, is a gentle, hands-on method of evaluating and enhancing the function of the craniosacral system ‚Äî the membranes and fluid that surround and protect the brain and spinal cord.

This therapy focuses on the subtle movements of the skull bones, spine, and sacrum to release tension, reduce pain, and promote overall well-being.

How Does Cranial Treatment Work?

The human body has a natural rhythm created by the flow of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) within the craniosacral system. When this rhythm is disrupted due to trauma, stress, or illness, it can cause a variety of physical and neurological symptoms.

During cranial treatment:

  • The practitioner gently places their hands on the head, neck, or spine.
  • They assess the rhythm and movement of the CSF.
  • Using light pressure (often no more than 5 grams), they help release restrictions in the craniosacral system.

Conditions Cranial Treatment May Help With:

  • Headaches and migraines
  • Chronic neck and back pain
  • Stress and tension-related disorders
  • Sleep disturbances
  • TMJ (jaw) disorders
  • Post-concussion symptoms
  • Sinus issues
  • Infant colic and breastfeeding difficulties
  • Anxiety and emotional trauma

Is Cranial Treatment Safe?

Yes. Cranial treatment is non-invasive, drug-free, and suitable for people of all ages, including newborns and the elderly. Because the technique uses gentle touch, it‚Äôs considered one of the safest forms of therapy available.

Benefits of Cranial Treatment:

  • Improves central nervous system function

Who Performs Cranial Treatment?

Cranial therapy is typically performed by:

  • Osteopathic physicians (DOs)
  • Chiropractors
  • Physiotherapists
  • Licensed massage therapists trained in CST
